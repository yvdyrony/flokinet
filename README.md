## The Locations and What Each One Gets You

FlokiNET runs data centers in **Iceland**, **Romania**, **Netherlands**, and **Finland**. Each location is genuinely different — not just a geography option.

- **Iceland** — The flagship privacy location. Strong constitutional free speech protections. Green energy (100% renewable). Slightly higher pricing, but you're paying for a jurisdiction that takes your rights seriously.
- **Romania** — The budget-friendly workhorse. Excellent bandwidth allowances, DDoS protection included, lower price per GB than Iceland. Great for high-traffic sites where raw performance matters more than jurisdiction.
- **Netherlands** — EU data protection law, excellent European connectivity. A solid middle ground.
- **Finland** — Reliable EU-based infrastructure with good connectivity.

FlokiNET is built for a specific crowd, and that crowd knows exactly who they are:

- **Journalists and media organizations** — who need their content to survive legal pressure campaigns
- **Whistleblower and document-sharing platforms** — where the server location matters legally
- **Privacy-conscious developers and businesses** — who don't want their hosting provider to be an accidental data broker
- **High-profile sites that attract DDoS attacks** — because they know FlokiNET won't quietly throttle them or upgrade them to a "DDoS protection tier"
- **Anyone operating in a jurisdiction where DMCA-style notices are routinely weaponized** — for whom an Icelandic or Romanian host is a feature, not a footnote

Long-term users tend to be quite happy with FlokiNET. Common themes in reviews: support is knowledgeable and technically competent, uptime across shared and VPS environments has been strong over multi-year use, and the privacy-first approach actually holds up under scrutiny. Some users note that weekend support is slower, and the no-refund policy is firm — both things FlokiNET is transparent about upfront.

The no-refund policy is worth emphasizing. There are no free trials, no money-back guarantees. The standard advice: start with a monthly plan on a small tier to evaluate performance, then scale up.
